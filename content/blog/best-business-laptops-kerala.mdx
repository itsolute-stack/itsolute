If you run a business in Kerala — a clinic, a CA office, a real estate agency, a small school — sooner or later you need to buy laptops. Maybe one for a new hire. Maybe ten for an office refresh. The question is always the same: which one?

We sell and service business laptops every day from our Parthas Lane workshop. Here's the honest guide we'd give a friend.

## What "business laptop" actually means

Skip the consumer models. Business laptops are a different product category — built to be repaired, supported under business warranties, and run reliably for 4-5 years of daily work. The main brands have separate lines:

- HP: ProBook (mid-range business), EliteBook (premium)
- Dell: Vostro (small business), Latitude (corporate)
- Lenovo: ThinkBook (small business), ThinkPad (premium)

A consumer laptop costs less but won't survive the same workload. Plastic hinges crack. Keyboards die after a year. Support lines redirect you in circles. The price difference between a consumer Inspiron 15 and a business Vostro is usually only ₹5,000-8,000, but the long-term reliability gap is much wider.

## Budget tier — ₹40,000 to ₹55,000

For data entry, accounting, browser-heavy roles. Reception PCs. Junior staff.

What to look for: Intel i3 (12th or 13th gen) or AMD Ryzen 5, 8 GB RAM minimum, 256 GB SSD minimum, 14" or 15.6" FHD display, Windows 11 Pro.

Solid picks: HP 250 G10 (workhorse, easy service, common parts), Lenovo IdeaPad Slim 3 (best display in segment), Dell Vostro 14 3530 (reliable, good after-sales).

Avoid the "best deal" Celeron or Pentium machine. They feel cheap because they are, and they'll bottleneck within a year as Tally, Chrome, and Windows updates pile up.

## Mid tier — ₹60,000 to ₹85,000

For accountants, consultants, sales staff, anyone who lives in Excel or runs multiple browser tabs all day.

What to look for: Intel i5 (12th or 13th gen) or AMD Ryzen 5 7000-series, 16 GB RAM (this is the meaningful upgrade), 512 GB SSD, 14" FHD IPS display, backlit keyboard, at least 8 hours of real battery life.

Solid picks: HP ProBook 445 G10 (best support process), Lenovo ThinkBook 14 G6 (best build quality), Dell Vostro 14 5440 (best value, especially on bulk orders).

The 8 GB to 16 GB RAM jump is the single biggest performance gain at this price point. Don't skip it to save ₹3,000.

## Premium tier — ₹95,000 to ₹1,30,000

For doctors using imaging software, designers, owners and senior leaders, anyone who needs to work fast for the next 5+ years.

What to look for: Intel i7 (13th gen) or i5 H-series, 16-32 GB RAM, 512 GB or 1 TB NVMe SSD, 14" 2.2K or higher display, longer-life battery, fingerprint reader, TPM 2.0.

Solid picks: Lenovo ThinkPad E14 Gen 5 (the no-fuss choice), HP ProBook 450 G10 (best keyboard and durability), MacBook Air M3 13" (premium, locked into Apple ecosystem).

The MacBook is excellent but think hard about your software stack. Tally won't run natively. Many Indian banking and accounting tools assume Windows. If half your team is on Windows, mixing Macs adds friction.

## What actually matters beyond the spec sheet

Most reviews focus on processor benchmarks. For business use, these matter more:

**Keyboard quality.** Your staff types 6-8 hours a day. A bad keyboard causes wrist pain and slows everyone down. ThinkPads and ProBooks lead here. Cheap IdeaPads and Inspirons are usable but not pleasant.

**Service availability in Kerala.** All three major brands have authorised service centres in Kerala but coverage varies by region. HP and Dell have the strongest networks in Kottayam, Kochi, and Thiruvananthapuram. Lenovo coverage is good in cities, thinner in smaller towns. We coordinate warranty for our hardware clients regardless of brand.

**Spare parts availability.** Matters for laptops you'll keep 5+ years. Mainstream HP ProBook and Lenovo ThinkBook parts are stocked across Kerala distributors. More obscure models can take 2-3 weeks for parts.

**Display height and stand.** A 1920×1080 FHD screen at 14" is sharp enough. The real issue is laptop height — most laptop screens sit too low for ergonomic use. Budget ₹1,500-3,000 per machine for a basic laptop stand. Your staff's neck and back will thank you in five years.
